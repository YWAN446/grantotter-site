Everything You Need to Find Funding

Six tools that take you from researcher profile to proposal-ready — in one place.

🔍

Generate Profile

AI searches Google Scholar, PubMed, ORCID, NIH RePORTER, and your faculty page to build a comprehensive profile — covering research expertise, grant history, financial parameters, and grant suitability as PI or co-investigator.

🎯

Match Grants

A three-stage pipeline — hard eligibility filters, rule-based scoring, then AI-powered alignment analysis — ranks your top 10 matches from federal funding opportunities with plain-language rationale for each.

👥

Profile Repository

Manage your own profile, build a preferred collaborators list, and search an institution-wide faculty pool (currently RSPH at Emory) to discover potential team members by research expertise.

💡

Concept Brainstorming

Select a grant and a research team, and GrantOtter generates 2–3 tailored proposal concepts — each with a working title, specific aims outline, innovation score, and estimated budget breakdown.

📄

Create Biosketch

Generate a draft NIH Biographical Sketch tailored to your target grant, covering all four required sections. Use the output to populate your SciENcv common form before submission.

📡

Weekly Funding Feed

Stay current with a weekly aggregated feed of new grant opportunities and agency announcements from NIH, NSF, and DARPA — updated every Monday.

How It Works

Four steps from researcher profile to proposal-ready.

1

Build Your Profile

Enter your name and institution. AI searches the web and generates a comprehensive profile covering expertise, grants, publications, and budget parameters.

➔
2

Review & Update

Download the profile, correct any inaccuracies — expertise scores, affiliations, grant history — and re-upload. The profile is the foundation for everything downstream.

➔
3

Match Grants

Set your filters and run the three-stage matching pipeline. Get your top 10 ranked opportunities with AI-written rationale, emailed directly to your inbox.

➔
4

Brainstorm & Write

Generate proposal concepts with your team, then produce a draft NIH biosketch tailored to your target grant — ready to fill into SciENcv.
