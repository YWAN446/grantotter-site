Frequently Asked Questions

How do I create an account?

Go to grantotter.streamlit.app, click the Sign Up tab, enter your email and a password (minimum 6 characters), and click Create Account. You may receive a confirmation email — click the link to verify your address, then sign in. See the Step 0 of the tutorial for full details.

I forgot my password. How do I reset it?

Password reset is not self-service at this time. Email us at grantotter42@gmail.com and we'll reset it for you promptly.

What information does GrantOtter need to generate my profile?

Only your name and institution are required. Providing additional details — your Google Scholar URL, ORCID iD, department, and a link to your faculty or personal website with an up-to-date CV — significantly improves profile quality, especially if your name is common. We also recommend filling in Developing Research Interests and Areas to Avoid to get better-targeted grant recommendations.

What does the generated researcher profile include?

The profile goes beyond a publication list. It includes scored research expertise areas, financial parameters (estimated salary, F&A rates, fringe rates), grant suitability as PI or co-investigator, active and past grant history, collaborator network, and publication impact metrics. It's the knowledge base that powers every other GrantOtter feature.

What types of grants does GrantOtter search?

The grant matching and brainstorming features currently cover federal funding opportunities — primarily NIH, NSF, and DARPA opportunities listed on Grants.gov. For non-federal grants (foundations, private funders, international agencies), you can paste the full grant information as plain text in the grant document field of the Concept Brainstorming tab.

How often is the grant database updated?

The grant database is refreshed regularly to include newly posted and updated funding opportunities. The Weekly Funding Feed aggregates new announcements from NIH, NSF, and DARPA every Monday.

How does grant matching work?

Grant matching runs a three-stage pipeline: (1) Hard filters eliminate ineligible grants based on your eligibility, deadline, budget, and mechanism preferences. (2) Rule-based scoring ranks the remaining grants across dimensions like expertise match, agency preference, career stage, and budget fit. (3) LLM evaluation reads your profile and each top candidate in depth, producing a ranked top 10 with plain-language rationale for each recommendation.

Can I use GrantOtter for collaborators or team proposals?

Yes. The Profile Repository lets you manage your own profile, build a preferred collaborators list, and search an institution-wide faculty pool (currently RSPH at Emory University). When brainstorming concepts or generating biosketches, you can select any profile from your repository as the PI or co-investigator — it doesn't have to be you.

What is the Power User workflow?

Download your GrantOtter documents (researcher profiles, brainstorming report, biosketch draft) and upload them into a general-purpose AI assistant like Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini. These files give the AI a rich knowledge base to work from, so you can develop concepts into full proposals through natural language conversation — drafting a Specific Aims page, writing recruitment emails to potential co-investigators, and more. See the Power User section of the tutorial.

What is the NIH biosketch output used for?

The biosketch tab generates a draft NIH Biographical Sketch covering all four required sections (Personal Statement, Positions & Honors, Contributions to Science, Research Support). Use it as a reference to populate your biosketch on SciENcv, the official NIH tool for creating and maintaining biosketches.

Is my data secure? How does GrantOtter handle my privacy?

GrantOtter is built on three pillars of trust:

🔓 1. Open Data Sources Only

We build researcher profiles exclusively from publicly available sources. Your private files never touch our system.

  • ✓  Uses only public sources — PubMed, Google Scholar, ORCID, faculty pages, NIH RePORTER, and more
  • ✓  No manual CV uploads or private document sharing required
  • ✓  Your private files stay entirely in your hands
🔒 2. Platform-Level Security

Account security is handled by Supabase, an industry-standard backend trusted by thousands of applications worldwide.

  • ✓  All user credentials and passwords are fully encrypted
  • ✓  Secure login protocols protect your account
  • ✓  Industry-standard encryption at rest and in transit
👁️ 3. Zero-Access Privacy

This is the most important one. GrantOtter operates on a zero-access privacy model for your generated content — your research ideas and intellectual property stay entirely within your control.

  • ✓  The platform cannot see your generated concept notes
  • ✓  Your matching lists and biosketches are never stored on our servers
  • ✓  Your intellectual property is never used for AI training or shared externally
